The Unlocking Method

Who

We are the Chalmers students John Luc and Johnny Hamnesj√∂ taking a course in Mobile Computing.

Where

This is a prototype developed for the course CIU176 - Mobile Computing, design and implementation that’s given at the department of Computer Science and Engineering at Chalmers University of Technology and the University of Gothenburg.

What

The theme for this project is Authentication and Access, which means we are to design an innovative solution for the purpose of unlocking.

Vision

Our vision was to create VALUE for the user - this means that we intended to design a tool that first and foremost was FUN to use, and secondly it should provide a sense of USEFULNESS.

Methods

What we did was to prototype quick and dirty.

First week

We started out ideating and trying to empathize with the user. What did we actually want to accomplish?

We intially tried to keep the scope tight, so we wouldn’t suffer from a feature creep. To this extent, we kept on with creating quick sketches and prototypes.

This above image showcases one of the initial prototyping sequences where we storyboarded a user story.

As we initially ideated the actions of the user we hadn’t asked us the question of WHY. We’ll come back to that later on in the process, but for the context it’s important to keep this in mind.

Knowing when to quit

For the first week, we ideated several design concepts. We had been assigned challenges to solve, such as DESIGNING FOR THE REAL WORLD / RESEARCH THROUGH DESIGN / ARTISTIC SOMETHING. For each of these, we ideated a concept based on this type of design research phase.

The prototype for one of our initial concepts got a higher fidelity prototype, where we tried to showcase both how the screens would work in reality, as well as the user flow.
